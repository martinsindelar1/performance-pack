Executive readout

The big five · per bucket

● Positives

  • Net Profit 7.04M CZK YTD (Jan→May). Margin held ~19% even as volume cooled in May.
  • March was the strongest organic month — 8.30M revenue with no promo. The brand carries weight without a discount crutch.
  • Subscriptions scaling fast — active subs 43→159, now ~2.7% of May order revenue. New recurring lever.
  • Returning customers carry the business — returning revenue 4.35M = 72% of May's mix.
  • AOV resilient — held at 1,304 CZK in May (+2% vs Apr) despite the volume drop. Pricing power intact.

● Negatives

  • New Customer CPA hit 914 CZK in May (+47% from Jan's 623, +12% MoM). Acquisition keeps getting more expensive — the #1 red flag.
  • May was soft across the board — revenue 6.01M (−28% MoM), orders 3,945 (−29%). Weakest month of the year.
  • Blended ROAS slid to a year low — 4.85 in May vs 5.45 in Jan. All paid channels cooled together.
  • Meta & Google efficiency eroding — Meta ROAS 6.4→5.1×, Google 21→15× since Jan. The two biggest channels both cooling.
  • New customer acquisition shrinking — ~2,267 (Jan) → ~1,341 (May), −41%, while CPA climbs. Volume + cost both moving the wrong way.

● Opportunities

  • Subscriptions at 159 active (skio) — ~2.7% of May revenue and climbing fast off a tiny base. Biggest untapped LTV lever for H2; push the subscribe-and-save offer.
  • Replicate the April playbook: 2+1 mechanic + fresh creative drove CR to 2.75%. Summer Prep (Jun, 15%) is launching — lean into it.
  • Meta is still the workhorse at 5.05× even in a soft May — a creative refresh should pull it back toward its 6.93× March high.
  • AOV resilient at 1,304 in May on a returning-heavy mix. Push cart upsell, bundles and quantity breaks.
  • Returning revenue is the engine (72% of May). Referral + Pro/subscription program compounds this — under-built today.
